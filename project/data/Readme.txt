LIMIT_BAL: Amount of the given credit (NT dollar): it includes both the individual consumer credit and his/her family (supplementary) credit.

SEX: Gender (1 = male; 2 = female).

EDUCATION: Education (1 = graduate school; 2 = university; 3 = high school; 0, 4, 5, 6 = others).

MARRIAGE: Marital status (1 = married; 2 = single; 3 = divorce; 0=others).

AGE: Age (year, continous).

PAY_1 - PAY_6: History of past payment. We tracked the past monthly payment records as follows: PAY_1 = the repayment status one month ago; PAY_2 = the repayment status two month ago; . . .;PAY_6 = the repayment status six month ago. 

The measurement scale for the repayment status is:

-2: No consumption; -1: Paid in full; 0: The use of revolving credit; 1 = payment delay for one month; 2 = payment delay for two months; . . .; 8 = payment delay for eight months; 9 = payment delay for nine months and above.

BILL_AMT1 - BILL_AMT6: Amount of bill statement (NT dollar, continous). BILL_AMT1 = amount of bill statement one month ago; BILL_AMT2 = amount of bill statement two month ago; . . .; BILL_AMT6 = amount of bill statement six month ago.

PAY_AMT1 - PAY_AMT6: Amount of previous payment (NT dollar, continous). PAY_AMT1 = amount paid one month ago; PAY_AMT2 = amount paid two month ago; . . .; PAY_AMT6 = amount paid six month ago.

Y: client's behavior; Y=0 then not default, Y=1 then default"
